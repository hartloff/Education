== Use Case Preparation
Use case preparation will be presented as a lecture activity which is due Wednesday April 4th @ 11:59pm. As a beta release requirement it will be due Wednesday April 11th @ 11:59pm. If your team did not satisfactorily complete the lecture activity you will have a chance to revise your work before the second deadline to avoid a grade decrease.

Describe 4 use cases for your project and provide testing procedures for 2 of them. This should all be added to a new wiki page titled "Beta Testing". These use cases will be tested by the 100-level students while they beta test your project. They will execute the testing procedures that you provide as well as execute their own testing procedures for the other 2 use cases.

Each use case will be a specific set of actions that the user will take in your program as well as the expected outcome. These use cases will be assessed on the following criteria:

  • User Identification: Did you clearly identify the specific user for which this use case applies
  • Related to description: Is the use case related to the description of your project (user story and feature set) in the readme of the project
  • Non­Technical Presentation: Is the use case described appropriately for a non-technical audience
  • Not ambiguous content: Is description of the use case clear with no room for interpretation

For the two testing procedures you will describe the specific ways that the use cases will be tested by your beta testers. The testing procedures will contain, and will be assessed based on, three separate sections:

  • Target: Clearly and specifically identify the aspect of the project being tested
  • Testing Procedure: The procedure itself that will be followed by your beta testers. This should include all the specific steps to be followed during testing
  • Expected Outcome: Clearly describe the expected behavior of the program after the testing procedure has been followed

Use cases and testing procedures will be assessed by an outside party using this rubric (Grading Rubric). If your team scores 1 or 2 on any of these criteria for any of your use cases or testing procedures your team will not earn credit for your submission.

As a example suppose we are writing use cases for the search feature on Amazon, or any online retailer with a search feature.

Examples of use cases:

  • A use case that would receive all 4's on the rubric
    • "As a buyer I will type a product name into the site’s search bar and initiate a search by clicking on the magnifying glass symbol on the right side of the search bar to find the item I search for with the results sorted by how closely it is to what I typed for the search."
  • A use case that would receive all 3's on the rubric
    • "As a user I will search for the product by initiating a search in the search bar and get results in an ordered list."
  • A use case that would receive all 2's on the rubric
    • "I will use the search feature to look for an item I want to purchase and get back an ordered list from the search algorithm."
  • A use case that would receive all 1's on the rubric
    • "Simply search for the item and, if you do it right, the algorithm will create a list of results."

Example of a testing procedure that would receive all 4's

  • Target:
    • The target is to test the process of searching for an item on Amazon using the name of the item as the search criteria.
  • Testing Procedure:
    • On the Amazon homepage (amazon.com) locate the search bar near the top of the screen. This will appear as a white rectangle where you can type and has a yellow box with a magnifying glass image on the right side of the bar.
    • In this search bar, type the phrase "The Matrix", without quotes, then click on the yellow box with the magnifying glass image.
  • Expected Outcome:
    • After following the testing procedure the page should now show results based on your search with the most relevant product at the top of the page. Verify that the movie titled "The Matrix" is the top result and that the two following results are "The Matrix Reloaded" and "The Matrix Revolutions" in either order (ie. it doesn't matter which is second and which is third as long as these are the second and third results).

== Deploy Beta Release

The beta version of your software must be available and accessible to a non-technical user. Your beta release can replace your alpha release and must meet the same requirements except it will be provided to your users through a landing page.

== Landing Page

Your beta release must be accompanied by a landing page for your project that includes instructions and details relevant to your beta testers. This site is separate from anything on the course website and you cannot assume visitors of this site know anything about your project. This implies that you cannot assume the users have seen any of your videos, though you can add any videos to your landing page if you want them to have that information.

This is the site you should/will share with anyone interested in the project and it can be thought of as a poster advertising your project. You can include any additional information you'd like to this page, but for the beta release you are required to have at least the following:

  • A self-contained description of your project
  • Instructions suitable for a non-technical user to access, run, and test your beta release
  • A clearly indicated link to your beta testing feedback form
  • A link to the issues page of your GitHub repository clearly marked as way to submit bug reports

You should also add any additional information that is specific to your project such as specific bugs to look for, which features to test, special testing credentials, etc. The more information you provide to your testers and the more specific questions you ask them the more likely you are to receive meaningful feedback.

*If your project is a web app the landing page can be the deployed app itself as long as it meets all the criteria of the landing page.

To submit, add a link to you landing page in the readme of your repository.

== Testing

Although your testing at the team level will not be graded directly, you will be held accountable for any bugs or errors that appear in your released software. For your beta release there may be many minor bug reports (this is the purpose of beta testing), but you should not release your beta version with any major bugs or broken features. The bugs reported by beta testers should be such that they would have been very difficult to find in development.

It is highly recommended that your team agrees on a robust testing methodology so you can release your software with confidence. In addition, robust testing will make code reviews much simpler for the team. At the very least, it will save the team a lot of time since you can limit the number of times you'll need to go through the Hot Fix protocol.
