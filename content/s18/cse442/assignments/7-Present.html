== Maintenance
Technical Requirement: No major bug reports.
Verify: The course staff will determine if any bug reports are considered major. A major will be defined as a bug that prevents users from using your software for its intended purpose (ex. Not all features are functional)
Technical Requirement: Interact with your users.
Verify: Course staff will verify that the developers are interacting with users who contact them. This includes responding to bug reports, feature requests, comments on GitHub issues, and messages on the team's Ryver forum.

Your deployed software must be available to users throughout this entire three week period. Users will expect your MVP and at least 1 add-on feature to be available and these features must work correctly for their intended purpose. Any issues with your software may be reported on GitHub issues and you must be responsive to any such issues. Users may be actively trying to break your software so watch for bug reports. Unlike the beta version, your released software is expected to be robust and bug free. If a bug does arise, the team must be responsive in addressing the issue and release an update if needed.

== Presentation
Technical Requirement: Present your project following all the requirements below.
Verify: Course staff will watch your presentation to verify that all criteria have been met.

Your team must schedule a time to present your final project and present. All team members must attend the presentation. The presentation will be assessed using the following criteria:

  • A self-contained description of the project must be presented. Anyone in the audience with no prior knowledge about your work should get a clear idea of what your software does and what it accomplishes through the presentation alone.
  • You must show a live working demo of the official release of your software.
  • The technologies use to implement your project must be covered. Unlike the YouTube videos, you will get technical in your presentation. This does not have to be detailed, but it should give the audience an idea of how your project was implemented. For example, describe which languages, libraries, services, and frameworks you used and how they interact with each other. You do not need to show code in your presentation.
  • The presentation must be executed professionally. There is no need to dress up, but you should be organized and well prepared before presenting. You should not be struggling with the projector, talking over your team members, figuring out what to say, debugging your demo, exceeding your scheduled time, etc during your presentation. Be prepared to show off your project.

Only team members in attendance for the presentation will receive the points above. Not all team members have to speak, but all need to be present and at the front of the room throughout the presentation. If a group member is unable to attend the team presentation time please notify the course staff at least 24 hours before the presentation. Excuses given later will not be entertained unless there was a documented emergency.

Note: As the lecture activities during this phase all students will be asked to rate the presentations.
