== Practical Objective

Objective: Develop a running prototype that, by itself (no documentation or video required), clearly and simply conveys the idea of the final product to a non-technical audience. The prototype must also be easy to run for a technical user. Instructions for running must be provided in the readme for the project (see the readme requirement of the MVP phase).

Verify: The course staff will "spin up" your prototype by following the readme and a non-technical user may be given the prototype and asked what it is supposed to accomplish. The users description must match the stated idea for the project, within reason. The non-technical user will not be given any information outside of your prototype (They will not be shown the readme, prototype video, project page, etc. Only the running code). If there is any difficulty at all in this process then this requirement is not met.
Note: Practical objectives will be very difficult to achieve throughout this course. Final grading decisions will be made by the course instructor and these decisions will be strict. Close does not count for these objectives.

== Prototype
Technical Requirement: Develop a running prototype of your project that shows the idea of the final product.
Verify: Course staff will verify that the prototype accurately shows your project idea. This verification is much less strict than the corresponding practical objective and may be checked through your video without running your prototype (ie. no readme required).
Technical Requirement: Create a release from the develop branch of your prototype on GitHub as "v0.1.0"
Verify: This release will appear on your project page.

In this phase your team will develop a prototype of your project. This prototype will be working software that runs and gives the customer a clear vision of your final product. No functionality is required and most of the focus will be on the visuals. For example, you might have buttons that do not perform any actions, but it should be clear what the button will do after the software is developed and released. This prototype will become your final product by the end of the semester after your team implements all the functionality.

To finalize the prototype it must be merged into your develop branch and be tagged as release "v0.1.0" on GitHub.

Note that any code contributed to the project must be made following the proper procedures. For code contributions this involves creating a feature branch off of develop, writing and testing the code, creating a pull request with the appropriate reviewer assigned, reviewing the code, and merging the code into the develop branch. This procedure will be covered in more detail in lecture. Any team member not following this and other procedures will receive a grade reduction.

== YouTube Video
Technical Requirement: Upload a video demo of your prototype to YouTube that is shorter than 1 minute.
Verify: This video will appear on your project page. Course staff will verify that it meets the requirements.

In addition to creating a release on GitHub you will also produce a video demo of your prototype and upload it to YouTube. This video must give an accurate demo of the current state of your project and show the running prototype. This video will be directed towards a non-technical audience to give people an idea of what you are going to accomplish this semester. You should not show any code or use any technical language in this video.

This video must be shorter than 1 minute. The goal is to quickly communicate your idea to the end users.


Submission

Once complete, submit the link to your prototype video here. After submitting, check your project page to verify that the video is displayed properly.
