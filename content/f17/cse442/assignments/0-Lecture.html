== Lecture

Many lectures will involve interactive activities. These activities are designed to help you understand and apply various aspects of software engineering. We will also explore software engineering as a whole and discuss it's role in society.

These activities will not exist exist outside of lecture and you must attend lecture to earn credit for them. Attempting to earn credit for an activity without being physically present in lecture is an academic integrity violation and [if caught] will result in a F in the course. A variety of methods will be used to detect violators. Do not test this unless you are comfortable with an F.

== Grading

Each activity is graded on a binary scale. You either completed the activity, or you didn't. The effect on your final grade will be determined by the number of incomplete activities with grade changes following the same scale as given in the syllabus. This will be a very rare case where you have a clearly defined way to improve your grade.

Incomplete Activities Grade Change
0 +1
1-3 0
4-5 -1
6-7 -2
8+ -3

*If the response rate on the course evaluations is at least 99%, all cutoff values for incomplete activities will be increased by 1. This would mean that missing 0-1 activities would result in +1, miss 2-4 for 0, etc.

== Make Up

For each activity there will be a make up opportunity for any student who attended lecture and attempted the activity, but was unable to successfully complete it. No make ups will be given to student who either missed lecture, or were present but did not make an honest effort to complete the activity.

The intent of these activities is to further your understanding of software engineering, not cause stress about completing each activity. Take your time, relax, and use the make up opportunities if needed.
