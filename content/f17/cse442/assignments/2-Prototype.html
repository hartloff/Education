== Prototype

Goal: Develop a demo of your software.

Your prototype will be working software that runs and gives the customer a clear vision of your final product. No functionality is required and most of the focus will be on the visuals. For example, you might have buttons that do not perform any actions, but it should be clear what the button will do after the software is developed and released. This prototype will become your final product by the end of the semester after your team implements all the functionality.

To finalize the prototype it must be merged into your master branch and be tagged as release "v0.1.0" on GitHub.

== YouTube Video

In addition to creating a release on GitHub you will also produce a video demo of your prototype and upload it to YouTube. This video must give an accurate demo of the current state of your project and show the running prototype. This video will be directed towards a non-technical audience to give people an idea of what you are going to accomplish this semester. You should not show any code or use any technical language in this video.

This video must be shorter than 1 minute. The goal is to quickly communicate your idea to the end users.


Submission

Once complete, submit the link to your prototype video here. After submitting, check your project page to verify that the video is displayed properly.

== Pitfalls
-1 | Did not complete prototype and release as "v0.1.0" on GitHub
-1 | No video submission, video does not show the working prototype, or video is longer than 1 minute
-1 | Prototype and video together do not give a clear vision of the final product

The following pitfalls will apply for the remainder of the semester (including this submission).

-1 | Individual: Not following the proper processes including the branching model, pull requests, code reviews, etc.
-1 | Individual: Failure to complete an assigned task. To be considered complete the code for a task must be reviewed, accepted, and merged into develop. If the code is part of the current release it must also be merged into master.

These points stack so if someone doesn't do anything for an entire submission [and they had a task assigned to them] they will receive a -2, or worse.
