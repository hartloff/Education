  • Maintain your deployed software
  • Address any bug reports
  • Present your project
== Maintenance

Your deployed software must be available to users throughout this entire three week period. Users will expect your MVP and at least 1 add-on feature to be available and these features must work correctly for their intended purpose. Any issues with you software may be reported on GitHub issues and you must be responsive to any such issues. Users may be actively trying to break your software so watch for bug reports. Unlike the beta version, your released software is expected to be robust and bug free. If a bug does arise the team must be responsive in addressing the issue that release an update if needed.

== Presentation

Your team must schedule a time to present your final project and present. All team members must attend the presentation. The presentation will be assessed using the following criteria:

  • A self-contained description of the project must be presented. Anyone in the audience with no prior knowledge about your work should get a clear idea of what your software does and what it accomplishes through the presentation.
  • You must show a live working demo of your software
  • The technologies use to implement your project must be covered. Unlike the YouTube videos, you will get technical in your presentations. This does not have to be detailed, but it should give the audience an idea of how your project was implemented. For example, describe which languages, libraries, services, and frameworks you used and how they interact.
  • The presentation must be executed professionally. There is no need to dress up, but you should be organized and well prepared before presenting. You should not be struggling with the projector, talking over your team members, figuring out what to say, debugging your demo, going over your scheduled time, etc during your presentation. Be prepared.

Only group members in attendance for the presentation will receive the points above. If a group member is unable to attend the groups presentation time please notify the course staff at least 24 hours before the presentation. Excuses given later will not be entertained unless there was a documented emergency.

== Pitfalls
-1 | A major bug was reported. Major is defined as a bug that prevents the use of your MVP or add-on feature in a common situation.
-1 | A minor bug was reported that was not addressed quickly, effectively, and appropriately
-1 | Any of the presentation criteria were not satisfied within the scheduled presentation time
-1 | Individual: No show at presentation
